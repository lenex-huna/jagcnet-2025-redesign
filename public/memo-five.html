It is with deep sadness that we inform you of the passing of Colonel (Retired) Thomas Patrick DeBerry of Marietta, Georgia. COL (Ret.) DeBerry passed away on July 1, 2024. He was 81 years old.

COL (Ret.) DeBerry was born at Camp Wheeler, Georgia, May 26, 1943. He was the only child of Thomas Graham DeBerry and Henrietta Wolfe DeBerry. He grew up in Morgantown and Fairmont, West Virginia. He attended West Virginia University and West Virginia University College of Law. After graduation, he joined the U.S. Army Judge Advocate General’s Corps.

COL (Ret.) DeBerry’s military career was long and distinguished, serving in the Vietnam War and at assignments at the JAG School in Charlottesville, Virginia, and Ft. Huachuca, Arizona, among many others. He especially enjoyed his tenure as the Staff Judge Advocate in West Berlin, Germany at the height of the Cold War. He received many honors and decorations including the Legion of Merit, the Bronze Star, the Meritorious Service Medal, and the Army Commendation Medal.

Following his retirement from the Army, he worked as an Assistant Professor of Law and Associate Director for the Institute for Continuing Legal Education of the State Bar of Georgia and later started a business that provided continuing legal education. He also opened his own law office in the Atlanta area and practiced for many years in the areas of business law, workers’ compensation, and veterans’ affairs.

COL (Ret.) DeBerry will be remembered for his sense of humor, keen intellect, and curiosity. He was a marksman, sailor, traveler, conversant in German, and an avid coin collector.

COL (Ret.) DeBerry is survived by his wife, Mary Wood DeBerry; three daughters and two sons-in law, Andrea Santos, Jill Bonds (Russell) and Shannon Emond (Josh); eight grandchildren, Meagan Santos, Sean Santos (fiancée Sky), Catherine Santos, Caroline Bonds, Sophie Bonds, Ava Bonds, Corey DeBerry and Nicholas Emond; and three great granddaughters.

COL (Ret.) DeBerry donated his remains to the Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine.

A memorial service will be held for COL (Ret.) DeBerry at a later date.

Please keep the DeBerry family in your thoughts during this most difficult time.
