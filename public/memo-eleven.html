Notice of Passing – Colonel (Retired) Burnett H. Radosh

It is with deep sadness that we inform you of the passing of Colonel (Retired) Burnett H. Radosh, who passed away peacefully on 26 March 2024 at his son’s home in Arlington, Virginia. He was 88 years old.

Burnett H. Radosh was born on 29 March 1935 in New York City, New York, to Louis and Esther Radosh, and was raised in Brooklyn and Neponsit, Queens. Burnett graduated with his Bachelor’s Degree from the University of Chicago in 1953, and his Legum Baccalaureus from the New York University School of Law in 1956.

After graduating from law school, Burnett Radosh joined the US Army, starting his Army career as an enlisted Soldier, with infantry training at Fort Benning, Georgia. He became a law clerk in the Fort Benning JAG office, and within six months of his enlistment was appointed a 1st Lieutenant in the US Army JAG Corps, undergoing JAG officer training at Fort Benning in 1958.

In 1961, Burnett moved to France on the USNS Patch, a troop ship. After two years in France, he was assigned to Charlottesville, Virginia, and Fort Liberty (Bragg), North Carolina. While in North Carolina, Burnett spent one year with the 82nd Airborne Division before being curtailed to go on his first tour to Vietnam. His second tour in Vietnam was with the 25th Infantry Division at Củ Chi. Following his second tour in Vietnam, Burnett was stationed in Northern Virginia, where he held various assignments, including working in the Pentagon for the Office of The Judge Advocate General. Burnett retired from the US Army on 30 June 1980.

Burnett is survived by his two sons, Alaric and Jeremy, and their families; his brother, Edward, and his family; and his nephew Lee and niece Laura, along with their families.
