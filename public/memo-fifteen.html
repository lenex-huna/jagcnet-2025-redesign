Notice of Passing – Colonel (Retired) Patrick Dennis O’Hare

It is with deep sadness that we inform you of the passing of COL (Ret) Pat O’Hare of Charlottesville, Virginia. COL (Ret) O’Hare passed away on 27 February 2024. He was 71 years old.

Pat O’Hare was born on 7 December 1952 to Joseph and Joan Brannon O’Hare. He obtained his Bachelor of Arts Degree from the University of Dayton in 1974 and his Juris Doctor Degree from Washington and Lee University in 1982.

Pat served in the JAG Corps for over 20 years, culminating in his final assignment as the Director of The Judge Advocate General’s Legal Center, TJAGLCS, Charlottesville, VA. He also served as the Director, Combat Developments Department, TJAGLCS, Charlottesville, VA; Staff Judge Advocate, National Training Center and Fort Irwin, Fort Irwin, CA; Regional Defense Counsel, Fort Lewis, WA; Deputy Staff Judge Advocate, 2d Infantry Division, Republic of Korea; Professor of Law, Criminal Law Division, TJAGLCS, Charlottesville, VA; Instructor, Criminal Law Division, TJAGLCS, Charlottesville, VA; Trial Attorney, United States Army Legal Services Agency, Falls Church, VA; Appellate Attorney, United States Army Legal Services Agency, Falls Church, VA; Trial Counsel, Combined Arms Center, Fort Leavenworth, KS; and Legal Assistance Officer, Combined Arms Center, Fort Leavenworth, KS. After his retirement from the military, Pat continued his career as a civilian at The Judge Advocate General’s Legal Center and School in Charlottesville, Virginia, where he retired in 2021.

Pat is survived by his beloved wife of 40 years, Sara Deaver O’Hare, son Patrick Andrew O’Hare, and his wife Emily of Richmond, Virginia. He is also survived by his brothers Joseph O’Hare III of Silver Spring, MD, Kevin O’Hare of Falls Church, VA, and Michael O’Hare of Montgomery Village, MD.

There will be a graveside service at Oak Grove Cemetery, 316 South Main Street, Lexington, VA at 11:00 am on 9 March 2024, followed by a Celebration of Life at University Chapel at Washington and Lee University in Lexington, Virginia at 12:00 pm on 9 March 2024 with a reception to follow.
