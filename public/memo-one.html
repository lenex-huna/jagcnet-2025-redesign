It is with deep sadness that we inform you of the passing of MSG (Ret.) Farley Darbasie of Temple, Texas. MSG (Ret.) Darbasie passed away on Saturday, August 11, 2024. He was 73 years old.

MSG (Ret.) Darbasie was born on July 15, 1951 in Trinidad to Cynthia Darbasie and the late Daniel Darbasie. He dedicated 22 years of his life to serving proudly in the U.S. Army, where he not only honed his leadership skills but also developed a deep appreciation for history and the arts.

MSG (Ret.) Darbasie enlisted in the United States Army in 1978 as a 71D Legal Specialist. His military assignments included II Armored Corps, Fort Cavazos, Texas; 25th Infantry Division (Light), Schofield Barracks, Hawaii; U.S. Total Army Personnel Command (PERSCOM), Alexandria, Virginia; and 3d Infantry Division, Katterbach, Germany. After retiring from the military, he transitioned into a new chapter as a tax preparer at Fort Cavazos, a role in which he continued to serve his community with diligence and care.

Beyond his professional achievements, MSG (Ret.) Darbasie's passions extended into the realms of art, history, and chess. His love for chess reflected his strategic mind, always seeking out the next move both on the board and in life, teaching others the beauty of patience and foresight.

MSG (Ret.) Darbasie is survived by his wife, Jenee Steadman; his daughter, Danielle; his mother, Cynthia; his sister, Dianne; and his brother, Curtis. They will all remember his kindness and humor fondly.

A memorial service for MSG (Ret.) Darbasie will be held at a later date.

In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to Fisher House in San Antonio to support military members, veterans, and their families.

Please keep the Darbasie family in your thoughts and prayers during this most difficult of times.
